Whitsunday Islands

About

The Whitsunday Islands sit in the heart of the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and are home to stunning coral reefs and diverse marine life, such as turtles, dolphins, and humpback whales.

The 74 Whitsunday Islands lie between the northeast coast of Queensland, Australia, and the Great Barrier Reef, a massive stretch of coral teeming with marine life. Most of the islands are uninhabited.

Hayman Island, one of the islands in the Whitsundays, is a private island and has attracted high-profile celebrities and politicians over the years, including Oprah Winfrey, Bill Clinton, and Nicole Kidman.
