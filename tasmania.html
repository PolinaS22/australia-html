Tasmania

About

The main island is roughly heart-shaped. The state owes its name to the Dutch navigator-explorer Abel Janszoon Tasman, who in 1642 became the first European to discover the island.

Tasmania is the only state in Australia that experiences significant snowfall during winter. Mount Wellington, located near the capital city of Hobart, often has snow-capped peaks from June to August.

The Tasmanian devil, a carnivorous marsupial found only on the island, is known for its aggressive and loud behavior and also for its endangered status.
